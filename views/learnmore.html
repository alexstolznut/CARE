Our Goal

Our team at Americare wants to inform veterans and service users about mental illness in a way that will encourage them to ask questions and to seek help if they need it. Americare welcomes all users, but wants those who are currently suffering from a mental illness and want support to learn more and to find the resources they need to get better. Veterans and Service Users who just want to support their fellow veterans as well can use the site to share their experiences and stories too (and even doctors if they wish to participate). Americare is a safe platform for anyone who has ever felt confused or alone while dealing with their mental illness. We want them to feel safe and to feel that they are not alone in their struggle.

Navigating the Website

Ask Questions

On the site, you can share stories, comments, or ask questions through the "Ask Questions" tab, which will take you to a forums page. On this page, you can post new threads, keep track of your threads, and keep track of your responses.


Explore our Map

By clicking on "Explore Map" tab of the navigation bar, you can find clinics in San Diego (more locations coming soon!) or you can explore different datasets about PTSD. You can learn how many are affected by PTSD in the United States based on locations. You can also see how many get treatment as well.


Learn About Others like You

Through the "Educate Yourself" button in the navigation bar, you can learn about others like you. There, it will show you statistics on how many veterans or service users that have a mental illness.


Other Information

Through the "Learn More" button, you can learn more about the team at Americare, our goal with the site, and information on how to use it.
You can logout of the site by clicking on the Logout button at the top of the site in the navigation bar.

Our Team

The people behind Americare are a team of undergraduate students at the University of California, San Diego who specialize in engineering and user centered design.
